Participant & recruit

Track every human in the trial — from first contact as a Recruit through lifecycle as a Participant — with a provenance-complete, bitemporal record that projects to FHIR R5 ResearchSubject, SDTM DM/DS, and CDASH DM without storing standards in the substrate.

Design boundary

USDM v4 has no per-subject runtime entity (verified: cdisc-org/usdm/src/usdm_model/ has no study_subject.py; SubjectEnrollment is aggregate-planned-quantity, not per-subject). Participant is execution-side — created by operational systems (eConsent, EDC, IRT, CTMS) when real humans enroll; it references Study, Arm, and VisitDefinition entities the USDM ingester previously wrote.

Primary cross-walk targets: FHIR R5 ResearchSubject • SDTM DM (demographics) + DS (disposition events) + IE (inclusions/exclusions) • CDASH DM.

Pseudonymisation boundary. cr:StudySubject is the pseudonymous in-study identity (defined in cr-core.ttl). hcls:Person is identifiable and stays inside the site sovereign boundary. The one legal bridge is cr:Enrollment (bitemporal, attested — see Enrollment & consent). Participant carries operator-vocabulary attributes (firstName, screeningNumber) for site-coordinator UX; PHI containment is enforced at deployment, not by the schema.

Operational hierarchy

Site → StudySite → Study → Protocol → SOA → VisitDefinition → ActivityTemplate

                    ↓ enrolledAt
                Participant → InformedConsentAct    (0..N; re-consent supported)
                    |         → ScreeningRecord      (0..N; re-screening supported)
                    |         → EnrollmentRecord    (0..N; typically 1)
                    |         → WithdrawalRecord    (0..N; multi-event)

StudySite is the anchor (1..1), not Site. Every Participant is enrolled at exactly one StudySite for exactly one Study. Multi-Study participation = separate Participant entities; cross-Study identity resolution is federation, not substrate.

SHACL invariants (Inv #43–#51)

#SeverityConstraint
43ViolationPost-consent state requires consent act. Any participantStatus in {ENROLLED…LOST_TO_FOLLOW_UP} requires ≥1 InformedConsentAct with consentStatus=OBTAINED. ICH E6(R3) §2.4.
44ViolationSCREEN_FAILED requires record. participantStatus=SCREEN_FAILED requires a ScreeningRecord with screeningOutcome=SCREEN_FAILED.
45ViolationRANDOMIZED requires arm and date. participantStatus=RANDOMIZED requires both assignedToArm and randomizationDate populated.
46ViolationWITHDRAWN requires consent-withdrawal. participantStatus=WITHDRAWN requires a WithdrawalRecord with withdrawalCategory=CONSENT_WITHDRAWN. WITHDRAWN is reserved for subject-initiated; investigator-initiated uses DISCONTINUED.
47WarningCOMPLETED needs date. participantStatus=COMPLETED should have completionDate populated.
48ViolationArm–Study consistency. Participant.assignedToArm must reference an Arm whose parent Study matches Participant.forStudy.
49ViolationStudySite–Study consistency. Participant.forStudySite must reference a StudySite whose forStudy matches Participant.forStudy.
50ViolationConverted recruit needs participant link. Recruit.recruitStatus=CONVERTED_TO_PARTICIPANT requires convertedToParticipant relationship populated.
51WarningStalled recruit. Recruit with contactAttempts ≥ 6 still in RESPONDED or CONTACTED states should be transitioned to DROPPED.

Recruit — the pre-consent realm

Recruit is a separate top-level entity covering the human in the recruitment realm, before consent. The recruiter is a distinct operator role (outreach / qualification / scheduling); their data shape is lighter (no consent, often only contact info + pre-screening flags); PHI boundary differs (HIPAA Safe Harbor easier to maintain pre-consent). Folding Recruit into a pre-consent participantStatus state would mix two operator workflows, two PHI regimes, and two analytics needs in one entity.

Recruit funnel state machine (10 states)

RESPONDED → CONTACTED → PRE_SCREENING_SCHEDULED → PRE_SCREENED → QUALIFIED_PENDING_CONSENT
                                                                               |
                                                                               v
                                                              CONVERTED_TO_PARTICIPANT  (terminal, success)
DECLINED | NO_SHOW | NOT_QUALIFIED | DROPPED  (terminal, attrition)

Channel ROI = count(CONVERTED_TO_PARTICIPANT) / count(all Recruits with same recruitmentSource). Funnel trajectory is queryable via NGSI-LD temporal property on recruitStatus.

Recruit → Participant transition

When the Recruit consents: coordinator creates a Participant with convertedFromRecruit pointing back. The InformedConsentAct's existence on the Participant IS the canonical conversion event. The Recruit record persists for funnel analytics. Recruit.convertedToParticipant must be populated when recruitStatus=CONVERTED_TO_PARTICIPANT (SHACL hard, Inv #50).

Twin-for-enrollment (deferred, v0.5+)

Real Recruits + synthetic-Recruits-from-twin populations + historical Participants from completed trials feed a unified twin synthesizer that predicts cohort composition, recruitment timeline, and drop-out risk before the trial opens. Enrollment is the most expensive bottleneck (80%+ of trials miss timelines; ~30% of trial cost is recruitment-related). The substrate decisions here (Recruit-as-separate-entity, participantStatus as temporal property) are the load-bearing pre-conditions. Twin synthesis is computational infrastructure for v0.5+ once Visit / Activity / Observation lift.
