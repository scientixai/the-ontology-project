Risk-based monitoring (RBQM)

A KRI breach at a single site triggers a chain of investigation, escalation, unscheduled monitoring, root-cause analysis, and CAPA. TOP makes that chain a graph — one query assembles it from signal to resolution.

Operator screen — KRI dashboard & signal lifecycle — GLP1-TRIAL-27

The impossible screen. “Show me the current risk posture across all sites — which KRIs are breaching, which site triggered an unscheduled monitoring visit, and trace that signal from the raw EDC data through the risk model to the CAPA being worked today.” Today: the risk management plan lives in a Word doc in the eTMF, KRI scores are computed monthly in Excel from EDC exports, monitoring visit records live in CTMS, deviation logs live in a separate module, and CAPA actions live in a quality management system. Connecting them for one signal takes a day of manual work. With TOP: one Scorpio traversal, one screen, 187 ms.

Why this view is impossible today. Risk-based monitoring requires correlating data across systems that have no native connection. The risk management plan lives in a Word document in the eTMF. KRI thresholds are defined in that document but computed monthly by a statistician who exports query-aging data from the EDC, SDV completion from the monitoring system, and deviation counts from the deviation register — and pastes them into Excel. A KRI breach triggers an email chain, not a graph query. By the time the email reaches the CRA, context has been lost. Connecting the breach to the CAPA to the effectiveness check three months later is manual institutional memory.

What TOP does differently. Every element of the RBQM chain is a first-class entity: cr:CtQFactor, cr:KRIDefinition, cr:KRIReading, cr:RiskAssessment, cr:MitigationPlan, cr:MonitoringVisit, cr:CAPA. The KRI threshold is a property of the cr:KRIDefinition — not a number in a Word document. A cr:KRIReading is automatically flagged when its value crosses the threshold property. The breach creates a cr:RiskAssessment entity linked via cr:assessedFactor and prov:wasDerivedFrom to the readings that triggered it. The monitoring visit mandate is a cr:MonitoringVisit entity linked to the assessment via cr:triggeredBy. The CAPA is linked to the monitoring visit findings. The entire chain is traversable with one join=inline&joinLevel=4 call.

The KRI matrix is a projection. The site KRI table above is not a dashboard maintained by a data team — it is a SPARQL projection over the latest cr:KRIReading entities for each site. When the next monthly reading is pushed to Scorpio, the table updates automatically. No ETL, no scheduled report, no stale PDF.

Regulatory traceability. ICH E6(R3) Section 5.18 requires that the sponsor’s monitoring plan be documented and that monitoring findings be traceable to corrective actions. In TOP, that traceability is structural: cr:KRIReading → cr:RiskAssessment → cr:MonitoringVisit → cr:SDVFinding → cr:CAPA. A regulatory inspector can traverse the same graph the clinical team uses — no separate audit trail report required.

  • HIGH risk assessment has no linked mitigation plan — ICH E6(R3) §5.18 requires documented mitigations for high-risk CtQ factors.
cr:OverMonitoringShapecr:MitigationPlanWarning
  • 100% SDV applied to a LOW-risk factor (over-monitoring; flagged for review against monitoring plan).

  • KRI reading breached its threshold more than 30 days ago with no linked monitoring visit or CAPA (unaddressed signal).
