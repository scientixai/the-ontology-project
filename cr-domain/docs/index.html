What this is

A ready-made, shared model of how a clinical trial actually runs — from a site's first activation through an End-of-Phase-2 decision. Instead of every organization re-inventing how to represent studies, subjects, visits, samples, consent and safety data, you inherit a model the industry can share, and your own data plugs into it.

Because it captures who did what, when, and on what evidence — and remembers every version of the truth over time — the documents you already owe (SDTM, USDM, a delegation log, an analysis dataset) come out as views, and a regulator's question becomes a query.

The value

Inherit, don't rebuild

Your graph starts with the industry's operator model already in place — the cold-start tax becomes a one-time inheritance.

One model, many views

CDISC SDTM/ADaM, USDM, FHIR, a delegation log, a data mart — all projected from one graph. The standards are views, not the source of truth.

Remembers everything

Every fact carries two clocks — when it was true, and when you knew it. Reconstruct any past state “as of” a date; back-dating is structurally impossible.

Provable by construction

Every assertion records who attested it, when, and on what evidence (W3C PROV) — attestation, not just a citation.

Safe & proportionate

Identifiable data is contained by design; compliance is graded by materiality; monitoring is targeted by risk, not blanket.

Organized by flow

Each sub-domain below is its own page — the screen, the data, the rules, the views — so you read only the part you operate.

Study & site set-up
Define the study and bring a site from feasibility to first-patient-in — with activation gated on its essential documents, and enrollment that can’t precede activation.Study start-up package
A site is awarded a study and receives a start-up package — not one file, but a set of typed documents (often downloaded one-by-one from a portal). The site's response splits into three work streams — regulatory, budget & contracting, clinical operations — each consuming package documents and producing site-specific outputs (customized ICF, the DoA log, a signed CTA). Regulatory + budget completion gates the SIV and activation.Delegation & attestation
The PI delegates each task to credentialed staff by attestation — and the delegation-of-authority log is a projection of those acts, not a maintained spreadsheet.Enrollment & consent
Bind a real person to a pseudonymous study subject (the only legal PII bridge), and obtain consent under a valid delegation.Schedule of Activities
The visit schedule most systems flatten into a 2D table with the timing buried in footnotes — modeled as a timeline: a planned template (windows, relative timing, ordering, repeats), projected onto a participant once the anchor is known, then reconciled against what actually happened.Data capture (EDC) & fidelity
Capture values against variable definitions (CDISC ODM EAV), verify by risk (SDV), and manage queries — with PHI classified at the source.Biospecimen & LIMS custody
Track a specimen’s chain of custody as a bitemporal state machine, its aliquot lineage, and assays — so any result traces back to the blood draw.Safety & pharmacovigilance
Adverse events and serious adverse events, with the expedited 15-day reporting clock measured bitemporally (awareness → submission).Risk-based monitoring (RBQM)
Target monitoring by risk: a high-risk critical-to-quality factor must be mitigated; 100% SDV on a low-risk factor is flagged as over-monitoring.Deviations & CAPA
The chain most CR models skip: a critical-to-quality factor is threatened by a latent operational condition (the antecedent — the missing middle), which manifests as a deviation, which triggers a CAPA. Model the cause, not just the event.Pre-IND gate
The front bracket: an IND-enabling narrative where every animal toxicity is addressed by a clinical safety assessment, plus the 30-day review clock.EOP2 & analysis
The back bracket: efficacy results that trace to their estimand and SAP (overstatement guard), the EOP2 readiness gate, and ADaM analysis traceable to native source.GCP & essential records
The ICH E6(R3) governance vocabulary no existing ontology owns — essential records, source data/documents, audit trail, certified copy, plus the institutional actors — cited to E6(R3), defined descriptively (the obligations live in the shapes, not the classes).TMF document binding
Classify a document to its artifact type (a SKOS scheme aligned to the DIA TMF Reference Model), then bind what its facts mean to the domain class — the comprehension layer the eTMF world omits.Roles, phases & actions
Who can do what, when — the operator-grounding layer that turns the reference graph into a system of record for authorization, delegation, and Jobs to Be Done.Interoperability
Owned, provenance-tagged crosswalks to external ontologies (verified IRIs), and every standard view the graph emits — the standards are projections, not the source of truth.

How to put it to work on your data

1

Map your entities to the model

Point your concepts at the shared ones (patient→hcls:Person, your subject→cr:StudySubject, a value→cr:ClinicalObservation). You align, you don't redesign.

2

Attach the envelope

ex:obs1 a cr:ClinicalObservation ; rdf:value "128" ;
    cr:forSubject ex:subj-001 ;            # pseudonymous, never a Person
    top:observedAt  "2026-03-10T09:00:00Z" ; # true-in-world time
    top:recordedAt "2026-03-10T09:05:00Z" ; # when the system knew it
    prov:wasAttributedTo ex:coordinator .
3

Validate — the graph catches PII leaks, undelegated work, orphan data, premature activation, unmitigated risk.

4

Emit what you need

Run a projection → SDTM, USDM, FHIR, the DOA log, a data mart, ADaM lineage — provenance intact.

5

Ask questions across time

As-of reconstruction, lineage walks, and bitemporal compliance ("reported within 15 days?") are queries, not reconstructions.

You brought thin data in your own shape; you got a validated, provenanced, regulator-ready graph — and the documents you owe come out as views.

Honest limits

  • Not here: agent orchestration, the runtime/broker — those consume the reference; they aren't part of it.
  • Pseudonymized, not anonymized: identifiable data is kept from flowing downstream, but statistical anonymization is an upstream/runtime responsibility.
  • Scope today: oncology, Pre-IND → End-of-Phase-2, plus the site-operations layer. The architecture generalizes; the content is bounded.
