TMF document binding

Classify a document to its artifact type (a SKOS scheme aligned to the DIA TMF Reference Model), then bind what its facts mean to the domain class — the comprehension layer the eTMF world omits.

Two bindings, kept distinct: document-type (“this is a Protocol Deviation Log”) and content (“its facts mean cr:DeviationEvent”). The commons owns the map (align + build); the consuming system classifies, extracts, and asserts prov:wasDerivedFrom at runtime — that seam is deliberate, and the runtime is out of scope.
