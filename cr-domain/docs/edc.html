Why this view is impossible today. A data manager asking “walk me through the Day 28 visit for SUBJ-0042” must open eight systems in sequence. The phlebotomist’s ASCP credential lives in CTMS. The delegation log linking her to the PI lives in a separate DoA system. The LIMS barcodes live in the lab informatics system. Cold-chain telemetry lives in the logistics portal. The LC-MS/MS result lives in the central lab’s LIMS. The PI’s e-signature lives in ClinSig. The open query lives in the EDC query module. Cross-referencing eight systems to reconstruct one subject’s visit takes hours per visit — multiplied across 149 participants and 12 visits each.

What TOP does differently. Every entity in this chain is a first-class NGSI-LD node in the Scorpio broker with a URN identifier: cr:SampleCollection, cr:Specimen, cr:CustodyEvent, cr:Delegation, hcls:Person, cr:AssayResult, cr:Query. Each relationship (cr:performedBy, cr:authorizedBy, prov:wasAssociatedWith) is traversable. A single join=inline&joinLevel=5 request to Scorpio returns the entire chain in one response.

URN identity scheme. Every entity in TOP carries a deterministic URN: urn:top:{EntityType}:{studyId}:{subjectId}:{visitCode}:{localId}. The blood draw entity is urn:top:SampleCollection:GLP1-TRIAL-27:SUBJ-0042:V03:PKB-001. The same URN can be referenced from the EDC query, the LIMS manifest, and the courier manifest — no join table required.

Bitemporality. Every entity carries two time axes: validFrom/validUntil (real-world) and observedAt (transaction time). The T=0 reference for PK is the validFrom on the cr:SampleCollection. If the phlebotomist’s delegation is later discovered to have lapsed, the audit query still returns the correct bitemporal answer.

One traversal to Scorpio assembles the entire visit chain — phlebotomist credential, LIMS barcodes, cold-chain, lab result, PI signoff, and open query.
